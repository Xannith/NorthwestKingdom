Governance Development

Lightweight Governance

The philosophy behind how Northwest Kingdom organizes itself.

The idea

Northwest Kingdom has always had a practical neighbor culture. People show up for work parties, share information, look out for each other's land, and figure things out together. That culture is worth preserving.

The goal of NWK governance development is not to build a bureaucracy. It is not to create paperwork for its own sake. It is to preserve what works, track what matters, and make responsibilities clear enough that the neighborhood can function well even as residents change.

What this means in practice

  • Document just enough. Write things down when forgetting them would cause harm or loss. Don't document things that don't need documenting.
  • Make roles visible. Know who is responsible for what. When roles turn over, the next person has what they need to pick up the work.
  • Preserve decisions. Record significant decisions and the reasoning behind them. A decision log is not a burden — it prevents the same argument from recurring.
  • Support new residents. When someone buys land in NWK, they should be able to learn what they need to know. That requires records.
  • Keep it reviewable. Anything written down should be revisable. Lightweight governance is not rigid governance.

What this is not

  • Not a corporate structure
  • Not a homeowners association
  • Not a replacement for the MLC
  • Not a source of authority over neighbors
  • Not permanent — this should evolve as the neighborhood decides

Current status
