Role definition

Records Steward

Institutional memory, meeting records, decisions, document versions, and archive integrity for Northwest Kingdom.

Draft role definition. This is a proposed role, not yet formally adopted. See the role discussion page.

Why this role exists

Institutional memory is not automatic. Without someone responsible for capturing decisions, meeting notes, and documents, neighborhoods lose track of what was decided and why. New residents have no context. Old disputes resurface because there is no record of how they were resolved. Good work disappears when the person who did it moves on.

The Records Steward is the role responsible for preventing that. This is a distinct role from the Technical Administrator because records curation — knowing what to keep, how to organize it, and how to make it findable — is different work from managing technical systems.

Core responsibilities

  • Take or collect notes from neighborhood meetings and publish them in the meeting notes archive
  • Maintain the decisions record — significant decisions and the reasoning behind them
  • Maintain the proposals archive
  • Maintain the vote record
  • Keep the document archive organized and annotated with version dates
  • Support the Technical Administrator in ensuring records are backed up and accessible
  • Support onboarding of new residents by maintaining current summary documents
  • Flag records that are outdated, conflicting, or missing

What this role does not do

  • Does not make decisions — only records them
  • Does not manage technical infrastructure (that is the Technical Administrator's role)
  • Does not represent NWK in MLC proceedings (that is the TC Representative's role)
